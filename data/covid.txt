The '''COVID-19 pandemic in Algeria''' is part of the [[COVID-19 pandemic|worldwide pandemic]] of [[coronavirus disease 2019]] ({{nowrap|COVID-19}}) caused by [[severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus&nbsp;2]] ({{nowrap|SARS-CoV-2}}). The virus was confirmed to have spread to [[Algeria]] in February 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Algerian health minister confirms first COVID-19 case {{!}} Africa Times|url=https://africatimes.com/2020/02/25/algerian-health-minister-confirms-first-covid-19-case/|date=2020-02-25|website=africatimes.com|language=en-US|access-date=2020-05-25|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200317052233/https://africatimes.com/2020/02/25/algerian-health-minister-confirms-first-covid-19-case/|archive-date=17 March 2020|url-status=live}}</ref> In December it was announced that Algeria will launch COVID-19 vaccinations in January 2021.<ref name="ewn.co.za">{{Cite web|title=Algeria to start Covid-19 vaccinations in January {{!}} Eyewitness News|url=https://ewn.co.za/2020/12/21/algeria-to-start-covid-19-vaccinations-in-january|date=2020-12-21|website=ewn.co.za|language=en|access-date=2020-12-27|url-status=live}}</ref>

== Background ==
On 12 January, the [[World Health Organization]] [https://www.who.int/ (WHO)] confirmed that a [[novel coronavirus]] was the cause of a respiratory illness in a cluster of people in Wuhan City, Hubei Province, China, who had initially come to the attention of the WHO on 31 December 2019.<ref name="Elsevier">{{cite web|url=https://www.elsevier.com/connect/coronavirus-information-center|title=Novel Coronavirus Information Center|last=Elsevier|date=|website=Elsevier Connect|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200130171622/https://www.elsevier.com/connect/coronavirus-information-center|archive-date=30 January 2020|access-date=15 March 2020}}</ref><ref name="Reynolds4March2020">{{Cite news|last=Reynolds|first=Matt|url=https://www.wired.co.uk/article/china-coronavirus|title=What is coronavirus and how close is it to becoming a pandemic?|date=4 March 2020|work=Wired UK|access-date=5 March 2020|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200305104806/https://www.wired.co.uk/article/china-coronavirus|archive-date=5 March 2020|issn=1357-0978}}</ref>

Unlike [[Severe acute respiratory syndrome|SARS of 2003]], the [[Case fatality rate|case fatality ratio]] for COVID-19 <ref name="Imperial13March2020">{{cite web|url=https://www.imperial.ac.uk/news/196137/crunching-numbers-coronavirus/|title=Crunching the numbers for coronavirus|website=Imperial News|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200319084913/https://www.imperial.ac.uk/news/196137/crunching-numbers-coronavirus/|archive-date=19 March 2020|access-date=15 March 2020}}</ref><ref name="Gov.ukHCIDDef">{{cite web|url=https://www.gov.uk/guidance/high-consequence-infectious-diseases-hcid|title=High consequence infectious diseases (HCID); Guidance and information about high consequence infectious diseases and their management in England|last=|first=|date=|website=GOV.UK|language=en|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200303051938/https://www.gov.uk/guidance/high-consequence-infectious-diseases-hcid|archive-date=3 March 2020|access-date=17 March 2020}}</ref> has been much lower, but the [[Transmission (medicine)|transmission]] has been significantly greater, with a significant total death toll.<ref name="WFSA">{{cite web|url=https://www.wfsahq.org/resources/coronavirus|title=World Federation Of Societies of Anaesthesiologists – Coronavirus|last=|first=|date=|website=www.wfsahq.org|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200312233527/https://www.wfsahq.org/resources/coronavirus|archive-date=12 March 2020|access-date=15 March 2020}}</ref><ref name="Imperial13March2020" />

== Timeline ==

=== February 2020 ===
On 25 February, Algeria laboratory-confirmed its first case of [[severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2]] (SARS-CoV-2), an Italian man who arrived on 17 February;<ref name=":0">{{cite web|url=https://africatimes.com/2020/02/25/algerian-health-minister-confirms-first-covid-19-case/|title=Algerian health minister confirms first COVID-19 case|date=25 February 2020|access-date=5 March 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200317052233/https://africatimes.com/2020/02/25/algerian-health-minister-confirms-first-covid-19-case/|archive-date=17 March 2020|url-status=live}}</ref> on 28 February, Algeria deported him back to Italy, via a special flight from [[Oued Irara–Krim Belkacem Airport|Hassi Messaoud Airport]] where he was subject to quarantine.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://echoroukonline.com/ترحيل-المواطن-الإيطالي-المصاب-بكورون/|title=ترحيل المواطن الايطالي المصاب بكورونا|date=February 2020|language=Arabic|access-date=5 March 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200310151221/https://echoroukonline.com/%D8%AA%D8%B1%D8%AD%D9%8A%D9%84-%D8%A7%D9%84%D9%85%D9%88%D8%A7%D8%B7%D9%86-%D8%A7%D9%84%D8%A5%D9%8A%D8%B7%D8%A7%D9%84%D9%8A-%D8%A7%D9%84%D9%85%D8%B5%D8%A7%D8%A8-%D8%A8%D9%83%D9%88%D8%B1%D9%88%D9%86/|archive-date=10 March 2020|url-status=live}}</ref>
